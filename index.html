PEGEL(7) · feature guide
the gauge
The big digits are the live water level in cm, next to the trend per hour (▲ rising · ▼ falling · ▬ steady) and the station's state relative to its mean low/high water. Stations that report them also show water temperature (WT) and discharge (Q). Data comes from PEGELONLINE (WSV) and refreshes every 5 minutes.
the living river
The cross-section waves at the real level: drifting current, a little ship, dashed marker lines for MNW / MW / MHW and all-time records (HHW / NNW). The scene reacts to the world — below MNW the bank cracks under a blazing sun; above MHW a storm rains into the swollen river; a fast-rising level sends a lone harbinger cloud in upstream; at ≤ 0.5 °C the river freezes over, floes drifting past a ship stuck fast. Real rain, snow, clouds and wind at the station draw themselves (via open-meteo), and after the station's actual sunset the real moon phase rises over twinkling stars — unless it's overcast.
history & archive
The sparkline starts with the API's data and grows: every visit merges new points into a local archive in your browser, so over time it covers more than the API can serve. Pick a window with the history chips (24H … ALL) — the choice lands in the URL, so shared links reproduce it. export / import / clear manage the archive; nothing ever leaves your browser. Want years instead of days? Picking 1Y / 5Y / ALL fetches the WSV raw archive (data back to 2000, condensed to daily min/max, hosted with this site and refreshed monthly) for the station on screen — marked as unvalidated raw data: outliers and gaps are part of the deal. The full archive link and import (which swallows the WSV ZIP directly) remain as the manual route.
elevation profile
Below the sparkline: the water surface elevation (m NHN) between the neighboring gauges on the same river, ordered by river km. The neighbors are one click away.
whole-river mode
Type a river name (e.g. RHEIN) or use ?river= to see the entire river as one longitudinal profile: every gauge at its live elevation — ◉ normal · ▼ low · ▲ high — plus a TROUBLE list of every station running low or high. Every marker, label and row is a click target.
the prompt
A tiny REPL. A bare name switches to that station (or river). Flags: --station, --river, --adsb, --history 24h…all, --export, --clear, --info, --help. Press / to focus it from anywhere; Esc dismisses help.
extras
Aircraft overhead from your own ADS-B receiver (--adsb URL) and real river traffic on the waterline from your own AIS receiver (--ais URL, AIS-catcher) — both URLs stay in your browser. The tab title and favicon carry the live level, dark mode follows your system, narrow screens switch to a 44-column compact grid, and the page installs as an app from the browser menu. Should a station ever sink into an error, a sea monster handles it.
